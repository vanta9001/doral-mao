The History of McDonald's

McDonald's began in 1940 when Richard and Maurice McDonald opened a small drive-in restaurant in San Bernardino, California. Their focus on speed, efficiency, and consistency quickly set them apart from other diners of the era.

In 1954, Ray Kroc, a Multimixer milkshake machine salesman, visited the restaurant and saw its potential. By 1955, Kroc opened the first franchised McDonald's in Des Plaines, Illinois, and laid the foundation for what would become one of the largest fast-food chains in the world.

Throughout the 1960s and 70s, McDonald's expanded internationally, introducing famous menu items such as the Big Mac and Egg McMuffin. Its golden arches became one of the most recognizable symbols across the globe.

Today, McDonald's operates in over 100 countries, serving millions of customers daily. It has evolved its menu to meet changing tastes while staying true to its original promise of quick, affordable, and consistent meals.

McDonald’s History Calendar

Year Milestone
1940 Richard and Maurice McDonald open the first McDonald's restaurant in San Bernardino, California.
1948 The McDonald brothers introduce the "Speedee Service System," revolutionizing fast food with assembly-line efficiency.
1954 Ray Kroc visits the McDonald brothers' restaurant while selling milkshake mixers and sees the franchise potential.
1955 Ray Kroc opens the first franchised McDonald's in Des Plaines, Illinois, marking the beginning of global expansion.
1961 Kroc buys exclusive rights to McDonald’s, securing full control and pushing rapid franchise growth.
1967 The first international McDonald's opens in Canada, followed shortly by Puerto Rico.
1968 The Big Mac debuts nationwide in the United States, becoming an iconic menu item.
1973 The Egg McMuffin is introduced, pioneering the fast-food breakfast category.
1975 The first McDonald’s drive-thru opens in Sierra Vista, Arizona.
1983 Chicken McNuggets are launched worldwide, quickly becoming a customer favorite.
1990 The first McDonald's opens in Moscow, Russia, symbolizing global reach at the end of the Cold War.
2006 McDonald's introduces healthier menu options, including salads, apple slices, and premium coffee.
2015 McDonald’s launches “All-Day Breakfast” in the U.S., one of its most popular modern changes.
2020 McDonald's adapts operations during the COVID-19 pandemic with contactless service and delivery.
2023 McDonald’s continues global expansion while investing in sustainability and digital ordering technology.
